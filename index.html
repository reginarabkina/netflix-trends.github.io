ANALYZING NETFLIX TRENDS THROUGH IMDb SCORES & ENTERTAINMENT CHARACTERISTICS

Introduction

Netflix once dominated the global streaming industry, but now numerous platforms compete for viewers' attention. Streaming has become the new norm in a way, as it begins to replace cable television, with channels moving content to their own platforms. New competitors such as Max, Peacock, Amazon Prime Video, Hulu, and many more have entered the streaming arena. While Netflix still holds power in its respective industry, how has the application changed across the years? With new competition, it becomes clear that the platform must continue expanding its library as it has done in the past, but does this mean sacrificing quality in exchange for quantity? Additionally, does it cater to the same audience it always has, or has that shifted as time has progressed? Our project aims to analyze these questions, as well as many more, regarding Netflix and how it has adapted with time.

Reference Papers

To learn more, you can visit the article Netflix, Library Analysis, and Globalization: Rethinking Mass Media Flows, which examines Netflix's 2021 data, revealing strategies like offering binge-worthy shows and releasing content in multiple languages to appeal to a global audience. Another article,“We Don’t Aspire to Be Netflix”, explores Netflix’s growth over time, highlighting its expansion to more countries, growing viewer bases, and use of user data for tailored recommendations. Both papers offer valuable insights into Netflix's evolving strategies, providing context for our analysis of Netflix trends and the changes observed in the data.

Introduction to the Data

For our analysis, we utilize two datasets from Kaggle: Netflix IMDB Scores and Netflix Movies and TV Shows. The merged dataset contains 10 columns and entries, spanning from 2008 to 2021. It provides a comprehensive overview of Netflix's content offerings during this period.

Variable Name Description
title Name of the movie or TV show
type Indicates whether the entry is a movie or TV show
release_year Year the movie or TV show was originally released
runtime Duration of the movie in minutes or average episode length for TV shows
imdb_score IMDb rating score (0-10) based on user reviews
imdb_votes Total number of IMDb votes received for the title
country Country where the movie or TV show was produced
date_added Date when the title was added to Netflix
listed_in Genres or categories associated with the title
rating Age certification rating (e.g., PG, R, TV-MA)

IMDb Scores by Media Runtime

This boxplot visualizes the distribution of IMDb scores for TV shows and movies, categorized by runtime length. The categories are:

  • Short: Runtime ≤ 90 minutes
  • Medium: Runtime ≤ 150 minutes
  • Long: Runtime > 150 minutes

Takeaways:

The analysis of IMDb ratings across different media lengths reveals several key takeaways. First, the average audience rating remains fairly consistent across short, medium, and long films. The median scores are all quite close, suggesting that simply making a film longer or shorter doesn’t guarantee a better rating. Additionally, the range of ratings, from lower to upper quartiles, is quite similar across all three categories, meaning no particular length consistently leads to more unpredictable or varied reviews. However, there are some distinctions in the presence of outliers. Shorter films tend to have the most extreme ratings, with both very high and very low scores appearing more often. Medium-length films also exhibit some outliers, but longer films tend to remain within a more stable rating range, indicating that short films may be more of a “hit or miss” with audiences. Interestingly, the highest-rated media are more frequently found in the short category, with a top rating of 9.3, slightly above the best-rated medium (9.2) and long (8.7) films. However, this doesn’t imply that short films are always better; in fact, there are also more very low-rated short films compared to longer ones.

Implications:

  • Runtime alone is not a strong predictor of success. Instead, other factors such as storytelling, production quality, and genre are likely more influential in determining IMDb ratings.
  • Investment in short-form content may yield higher highs, but it does not guarantee better ratings overall. While some of the highest-rated films are short, this category also sees more extreme reviews, both positive and negative. If we invest in short content, it should be carefully crafted to maximize impact and avoid pitfalls that lead to low ratings.
  • A diverse content strategy remains valuable, as high and low ratings exist across all runtime categories. Offering a mix of short, medium, and long content is a smart approach. This way, we can capture different audience preferences while reducing risk.

IMDb Score Trends Over Time

While runtime offers insight into audience preferences, it’s only part of the story. Netflix’s content strategy has evolved over the years, influencing the types of films and shows released and how they are received. The interactive visualization below explores these shifts by tracking IMDb scores over time, revealing trends in content quality and viewer reception. Hover over each point to see the average IMDb score per year and the year content was added to Netflix.

Takeaways:

Netflix’s content strategy has evolved in response to changing industry dynamics. Initially, the platform focused on acquiring high-rated content to build credibility, then shifted toward mass content expansion to attract a broader audience. Over time, Netflix refined its approach by balancing quality with quantity. A key moment in this evolution occurred between 2013 and 2014, when the rebound in IMDb scores highlighted the impact of Netflix’s investment in original programming. High-quality, exclusive content like House of Cards and Orange is the New Black became central to Netflix’s identity, helping it stand out in an increasingly competitive market. However, Netflix's fluctuating IMDb scores also reflect the tension between content volume and quality. While rapid expansion led to a temporary decline in ratings, the company ultimately stabilized its approach by curating a diverse library that maintains a consistent level of quality while catering to a global audience. These shifts in strategy are closely aligned with changes in the streaming landscape. As competitors emerged, Netflix responded by scaling its content library, then refocusing on originals, and finally balancing both strategies to sustain its market dominance.

Implications:

  • Netflix’s success hinges on its ability to adapt to audience preferences and industry shifts. Streaming platforms must stay agile, analyzing viewer behavior to refine their offerings.
  • The rise of original programming highlights the need for exclusive, high-quality content to stay competitive. Platforms that neglect strong originals risk losing engagement and brand loyalty.
  • Balancing content volume and quality is crucial—too much variety can dilute brand identity, while a narrow selection may limit reach. A well-curated, diverse catalog appeals to different audience segments.
  • Trends in IMDb scores suggest Netflix fine-tunes its strategy using audience insights. Other platforms can follow suit by leveraging data analytics to optimize content acquisition and production.
  • As streaming evolves, success will depend on anticipating consumer expectations, maintaining high production standards, and differentiating through compelling storytelling.

Average IMDb Score by Age Rating

The aim of this section of our analysis is to gain insight into the relationship between the age rating on content and viewer engagement.

The figure is a horizontal bar chart with color encoding. Each bar represents the average IMDb score of titles within a specific age rating, while the bar color reflects the total number of votes received — one possible measure of popularity. Hover over the bars to see the metrics.

Takeaways

  • As the level of maturity increases, the amount of votes on that level of content is greater. This is the case until the maturity level R. NC-17 has significantly less votes than the rest of the age ratings due to the age certifications being far less popular than the rest listed.
  • Average score is fairly consistent across the various age certifications, despite the frequency of votes all being greatly different.
  • Content rated TV-Y, TV-Y7, TV-G, and G tend to have fewer total votes and slightly lower average scores. This potentially means less IMDb user engagement with content aimed at very young viewers — perhaps due to IMDb’s adult-skewing user base.
  • NC-17 ratings have a small presence in Netflix content. This category appears with low scores and vote counts, indicating it may represent niche or less mainstream content with little access or appeal.

Implications:

  • Streaming platforms and production companies should consider the larger engagement seen with PG-13, R, and TV-MA rated content when planning releases or marketing strategies. These categories clearly resonate more with IMDb’s user base, suggesting adult-oriented content garners more interaction and discussion.
  • The lower vote counts for youth-rated content reflect IMDb’s likely adult-leaning user demographic. Platforms targeting families or younger audiences may find that IMDb ratings don't fully capture engagement from their core viewers and should rely on alternative metrics.
  • The sharp drop-off in votes for NC-17 content suggests a limited audience and potentially reduced discoverability. This could impact content placement decisions for media that falls into that rating, especially on mainstream platforms like Netflix.
  • Since age ratings correlate with vote volume, developers and researchers should be cautious using IMDb data as a proxy for popularity or quality across all age groups. The data may overrepresent certain segments (like adult viewers) and underrepresent others (like children or families).
  • While average score may be very similar, the popularity difference across maturity levels indicates that we should look further into the individual distributions for each age rating.

IMDb Ratings vs Number of Reviews

Takeaways:

The scatterplot reveals a potential relationship between the IMDb rating and the number of reviews (votes) a movie or TV show has received.

Through this visualization we can see that movies and TV shows with higher ratings often tend to have a larger number of reviews, especially if they are well-known or widely appreciated by the audience. This indicates that the most popular and highly rated content tends to accumulate more reviews over time.

In addition, there may be some content with high IMDb ratings but fewer reviews, possibly indicating niche content, older movies, or shows that were well-received by a smaller audience.

Implications:

A higher number of reviews might correlate with greater audience engagement. Content that generates more reviews could indicate that it is widely discussed, analyzed, or critiqued, suggesting it is popular or has a passionate fanbase.

For example, if a film or series has a high rating but low votes, it may indicate that while the content is well-received, it has not reached a large audience or lacks the viral quality that drives more people to review it.

Conclusion

The analysis of IMDb ratings across various factors, including media length, Netflix's content strategy, maturity levels, and audience engagement, has revealed several valuable insights.

Key Findings

  • Media Length and Ratings: Runtime alone is not a strong predictor of audience reception. Short films have a broader range of ratings, making them riskier investments, while longer films generally receive more consistent scores.
  • Netflix’s Content Strategy: Netflix has shifted from acquiring highly rated content to balancing quantity and quality, with original programming becoming a key driver of success.
  • Maturity Ratings and Audience Engagement: PG-13, R, and TV-MA content receive the most IMDb votes, while youth-oriented and NC-17 content tend to see lower engagement, possibly due to IMDb’s adult-skewed user base.
  • Ratings and Review Volume: Highly rated content often garners more reviews, whereas some niche content may receive high ratings but have limited audience reach.
  • Trends Over Time: IMDb rating fluctuations reflect industry shifts, streaming expansion, and changing audience preferences. The number of releases per year aligns with platform growth and major entertainment trends.

Limitations

  • IMDb’s user base may not fully represent all audiences, especially for family-friendly or niche content.
  • Regional differences are not accounted for, which could influence rating trends based on cultural preferences.
  • Correlation does not imply causation—high ratings do not necessarily lead to high engagement and vice versa.
  • Additional quality metrics, such as audience retention and social media engagement, are not considered.

Future Recommendations

  • Streaming platforms should balance quality and quantity by leveraging data-driven insights.
  • High-rated but low-review content presents an opportunity for targeted marketing to increase awareness and engagement.
  • Future analyses could incorporate additional engagement metrics, such as watch time and review sentiment, for a more comprehensive understanding.
  • Segmenting ratings by geographic region could help tailor content strategies for diverse audiences.
  • Continuous monitoring of industry trends and audience behaviors is crucial for adapting to technological advancements in the entertainment landscape.

By leveraging these insights, content creators and streaming platforms can optimize their strategies to sustain long-term success and maximize audience engagement.
